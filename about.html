Since 2014

We started Almanac because packages were the problem, not the solution.

Two friends, both former guides, tired of watching good itineraries get flattened into identical departure dates. Eleven years later, the studio is still small on purpose.

Our Position

A planner should know the place better than the client does — always.

Every Almanac planner lives, or has lived for years, in the region they plan. We don't hire generalists who can quote you Bali one week and Iceland the next. We hire people who have already spent a decade somewhere and simply write down what they know.

That's slower to scale. It's also the entire reason clients come back.

What we won't do

Three rules the studio has never broken, even when it cost us business.

01

No fixed departures

Every itinerary is built around your calendar. We have never once told a client to change their dates to fit ours.

02

No planner covers two regions

Depth over range. A planner who knows one place completely will always beat one who knows five places adequately.

03

No more than one trip a month, per planner

This is the rule clients notice least and that protects the work the most. Rushed planners write generic itineraries.
